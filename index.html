📋 Malaria Recognition & Preventative Environmental Deployment Guide

🌡️ Uncomplicated Malaria (Early Signs)

Symptoms typically appear 10–15 days following transmission bites:

  • Cyclical Fevers: Sudden high temperature spikes accompanied by intense shivering.
  • Profuse Sweating Stages: Heavy diaphoresis as fevers periodically break.
  • Somatic Discomfort: Severe headaches, muscle pain, nausea, and general systemic fatigue.

🚨 Severe Malaria (Emergency Indicators)

CRITICAL PROTOCOL: Seek immediate professional clinical attention if any signs present:

  • Cerebral Malaria: Convulsions, confusion, delirium, or loss of responsiveness.
  • Severe Anemia: Extreme lethargy coupled with laboured respiratory distress.
  • Systemic Damage: Jaundice (yellow eyes/skin) or an absolute inability to retain fluids.
